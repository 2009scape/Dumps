Controls - Display Options

Introduction


Introduction

To access your display settings, go to the 'Game Options' tab and select 'Display Options'. This will enable you to adjust the settings you have access to in your detail settings. For instance, if you are running RuneScape in high detail, you will be able to adjust all of your settings except how many levels are visible and whether there are ground textures.

The display options menu for high detail
You are able to adjust any settings with brighter orange text. Depending on the detail level you have selected, some options may be unavailable, while others will have a greater or lesser range of settings. The main buttons along the top of the interface can be used to set the game to standard or high detail, as well as controlling the size of your game window.

  • Visible Levels
    This option allows you to choose whether or not to see the levels above and below you in certain areas of RuneScape. If this is turned off, you will see black areas above and below you instead.

  • Remove Roofs
    You can turn this to 'Always' or 'Selectively'. If it is set to 'Always', you will not see the roofs of any buildings unless they are on a level below you. If it is set to 'Selectively', roofs will only be removed from buildings when they would obscure your view of your character (i.e. when you enter a building, or tilt the camera to put a roof in the way).

  • Ground Decoration
    Ground decoration includes small stones, long grass and the like. You might like to remove these if you find that the game runs slowly, although they add a great deal of extra detail to any area.

  • Texture Detail
    This controls the level of detail that your computer renders in high detail mode. Obviously, setting this to 'High' will ensure that the world of RuneScape looks as good as possible, but a 'Low' setting may improve how well the game runs on your computer.

  • Idle Animations
    If set to 'Many', this will switch off the idle animations for players when there are over 200 players within the minimap area. When set to 'Few', it switches off idle animations when there are more than 50 players within the minimap area. Idle animations are used when your character is standing and doing nothing.

  • Flickering Effects
    Flames in RuneScape, like flames in the real-world, often dim and flicker as they burn. Turning this on allows various lights to behave in the same way.

  • Ground Textures
    This option is not available in high detail. It switches off some textures on the ground to increase frame rate at the expense of visual quality.

  • Character Shadows
    This will toggle shadows for player and non-player characters. Turning this on will increase the realism of the game world, turning it off will increase the frame-rate.

  • Scenery Shadows
    You can switch scenery shadows between 'Dynamic', 'Static' and 'Off'. 'Dynamic' shadows casts shadows from all objects and matches their movement, 'Static' casts shadows that do not move, and 'Off' removes all shadows cast from objects in the game world. This option only applies to high detail.

  • Lighting Detail
    This controls specular highlights - which allows smooth or shiny surfaces (like armour) to appear to reflect light - and light sources (such as lanterns and fires). Turning lights on will have a large effect on the game world, where torches cast light in spheres as they do in the real world. Turning this option off will return to the old style animated lights.

  • Water Detail
    You can choose between high and low detail water. High detail water allows you to see the new riverbeds and other objects below the surface of the water, but is the most likely to negatively affect your frame-rate. Low detail water cannot be seen through (so things that are under the water do not need to be drawn), but you'll get the new water surface effects like rippled and subtle movement. If you are not playing RuneScape in high detail mode, water will be textured but not animated. In safe mode, water will be a flat colour.

  • Fog
    This doesn't turn the area fog off, it simply pushes it into the back of your game screen. You might want to do this if you are in a foggy area and you think it is obstructing your view of your character.

  • Anti-aliasing
    This defaults to ‘off’ but you can select 2x or 4x anti-aliasing if you want. Anti-aliasing smooths out the image when you are running in fullscreen, getting rid of jagged edges, for instance. The higher the multiplier, the smoother the image but the more work your graphics card must do for each frame.
